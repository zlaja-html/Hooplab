Session details

Tryouts

Evaluation that reflects the real game.

Selection is based on consistency, decision-making, effort, coachability, and performance under pressure. Tryouts are how HoopLab scouts players for future tours.

  • Three-day format with limited roster spots
  • Skill work, competitive drills, and controlled scrimmages
  • Feedback from coaches, scouts, and player-development staff
  • Selected players can be invited to join HoopLab tour rosters
Apply for tryouts

Tours

Tours are earned through the tryout process.

Players do not apply directly for tours as a shortcut. The tryout is the scouting stage, and HoopLab uses it to identify players who can represent the group in real club environments.

  • Tryouts help us scout players for tour selection
  • Selected players compete against club and semi-professional teams
  • Tour rosters are built around role fit, attitude, and performance
  • Game film and exposure are used to support market positioning

Individual workouts

Book focused development time with HoopLab.

Individual workouts are for players who want direct coaching, sharper habits, and training that translates into live games and future tryout performance.

  • Position-specific skill development and decision-making
  • Game-speed finishing, footwork, shot creation, and reads
  • Small-group or one-on-one sessions depending on availability
  • Built-in appointment booking for available workout times
Book a workout

Group sessions

Train in a competitive group setting.

Group sessions are built for players who want game-speed reps, accountability, and direct coaching in a small-group environment. Sessions run once or twice a month based on gym availability and roster demand.

  • Small-group skill work with competitive live reps
  • Decision-making, spacing, finishing, and shot creation
  • Session dates are posted individually, not as a weekly schedule
  • Players book available monthly dates through the application form

Player success story

Xavier Boyd used the pathway to move up.

After arriving in Germany, Xavier joined the Boeblingen Panthers and used that platform to adapt, earn minutes, and prove his value in a structured club environment.

With consistent performance, targeted exposure, and active communication in the German basketball market, interest from higher-level clubs followed. After half a season, Xavier secured a move to Kirchheim, a Regional League program.

We do not promise shortcuts. We build pathways.
